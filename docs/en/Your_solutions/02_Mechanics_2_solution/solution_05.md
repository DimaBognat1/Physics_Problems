# 5. Inelastic Collision

We are given:

m₁ = 70 kg, v₁ = 3 m/s  
m₂ = 140 kg, v₂ = 0  

---

## Solution

### Step 1: Conservation of momentum

For a perfectly inelastic collision:

m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v  

v = (m₁v₁) / (m₁ + m₂)  

v = (70 × 3) / (70 + 140) = 210 / 210 = 1 m/s  

---

### Step 2: Kinetic energy

Initial:

E₁ = (1/2)m₁v₁² = 0.5 × 70 × 9 = 315 J  

Final:

E₂ = (1/2)(210)(1²) = 105 J  

Energy decreased → not conserved  

---

## Final Answer

v = 1 m/s  
Kinetic energy is not conserved
