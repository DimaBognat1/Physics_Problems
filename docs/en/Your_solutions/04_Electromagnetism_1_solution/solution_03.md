# 3. Electrostatic Equilibrium

## Given

A charge

q₃ = +1 C

is placed on the line between two positive charges:

q₁ = +4 C  
q₂ = +9 C

The distance between q₁ and q₂ is:

L = 2 m

We need to find the equilibrium position of q₃.

---

## Step 1: Physical idea

Since q₁ and q₂ are both positive, they both repel q₃.

For q₃ to be in equilibrium, the repulsive force from q₁ must be equal in magnitude to the repulsive force from q₂.

Because the forces act in opposite directions only between the charges, the equilibrium point must lie somewhere between q₁ and q₂.

---

## Step 2: Define distances

Let the distance from q₁ to q₃ be:

x

Then the distance from q₂ to q₃ is:

2 - x

---

## Step 3: Write Coulomb forces

The force exerted by q₁ on q₃ is:

F₁ = k q₁ q₃ / x²

The force exerted by q₂ on q₃ is:

F₂ = k q₂ q₃ / (2 - x)²

At equilibrium:

F₁ = F₂

So:

k q₁ q₃ / x² = k q₂ q₃ / (2 - x)²

---

## Step 4: Simplify the equation

The constants k and q₃ appear on both sides, so they cancel:

q₁ / x² = q₂ / (2 - x)²

Substitute q₁ = 4 and q₂ = 9:

4 / x² = 9 / (2 - x)²

Cross-multiply:

4(2 - x)² = 9x²

---

## Step 5: Solve the equation

Take square roots of both sides:

2(2 - x) = 3x

Now solve:

4 - 2x = 3x

4 = 5x

x = 4/5 = 0.8 m

---

## Step 6: Interpret the result

So the equilibrium point is:

0.8 m from q₁

and therefore:

2 - 0.8 = 1.2 m from q₂

This makes sense because q₂ = 9 C is stronger than q₁ = 4 C, so the equilibrium point must be closer to the smaller charge q₁.

---

## Final Answer

The equilibrium position of q₃ is:

0.8 m from q₁  
1.2 m from q₂

along the line between the two charges.
