# 2. Electric Potential

## Given

Four point charges are placed at the corners of a square of side:

a = 1.0 m

The charges are:

q₁ = +1 C  
q₂ = −2 C  
q₃ = +3 C  
q₄ = −4 C

We need to find the electric potential at the center of the square.

---

## Step 1: Formula for electric potential

The electric potential produced by one point charge is:

V = kq / r

where:

- k = 9 × 10^9 N·m²/C²
- q is the charge
- r is the distance from the charge to the point

Since electric potential is a scalar quantity, the total potential is the sum of the individual potentials:

V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃ + V₄

---

## Step 2: Distance from the center to each corner

For a square of side a, the diagonal is:

d = a√2

The center is at half the diagonal, so the distance from the center to any corner is:

r = d / 2 = a√2 / 2

Since a = 1.0 m:

r = √2 / 2 m

---

## Step 3: Write the total potential

All four charges are at the same distance from the center, so we can factor out k/r:

V_total = k/r × (q₁ + q₂ + q₃ + q₄)

Now add the charges:

q₁ + q₂ + q₃ + q₄ = 1 − 2 + 3 − 4 = -2 C

So:

V_total = k/r × (-2)

---

## Step 4: Substitute the value of r

We have:

r = √2 / 2

Therefore:

V_total = (9 × 10^9) / (√2 / 2) × (-2)

Dividing by (√2 / 2) is the same as multiplying by (2 / √2):

V_total = 9 × 10^9 × (-2) × 2/√2

V_total = -36 × 10^9 / √2

Now simplify numerically:

√2 ≈ 1.414

V_total ≈ -36 × 10^9 / 1.414

V_total ≈ -25.5 × 10^9 V

---

## Explanation

The electric potential at the center depends on the algebraic sum of the charges because potential is a scalar.

Some charges are positive and some are negative, so they partially cancel.

The total charge contribution is negative, so the potential at the center is also negative.

---

## Final Answer

The electric potential at the center of the square is:

V ≈ -2.55 × 10^10 V
