# 9. Vector Lorentz Force

## Given

A proton moves with velocity:

v = (2i − 4j + k) m/s

in a magnetic field:

B = (i + 2j − k) T

We need to find the magnitude of the magnetic force acting on the proton.

For a proton:

q = e = 1.6 × 10^(-19) C

---

## Step 1: Formula for magnetic force

The magnetic Lorentz force is:

F = q(v × B)

So first we need to calculate the cross product:

v × B

---

## Step 2: Write the vectors in component form

v = (2, -4, 1)

B = (1, 2, -1)

Now compute:

v × B =
|  i    j    k |
|  2   -4    1 |
|  1    2   -1 |

---

## Step 3: Compute the determinant

For the i-component:

i[(-4)(-1) - (1)(2)] = i(4 - 2) = 2i

For the j-component:

-j[(2)(-1) - (1)(1)] = -j(-2 - 1) = 3j

For the k-component:

k[(2)(2) - (-4)(1)] = k(4 + 4) = 8k

So:

v × B = (2, 3, 8)

---

## Step 4: Find the magnitude of v × B

|v × B| = √(2² + 3² + 8²)

= √(4 + 9 + 64)

= √77

---

## Step 5: Multiply by the charge

The magnitude of the magnetic force is:

|F| = q |v × B|

So:

|F| = (1.6 × 10^(-19)) √77

Now approximate:

√77 ≈ 8.775

Therefore:

|F| ≈ 1.6 × 10^(-19) × 8.775

|F| ≈ 1.40 × 10^(-18) N

---

## Explanation

The magnetic force depends on the vector cross product of velocity and magnetic field.

That means:
- the force is perpendicular to both v and B
- we use the determinant to find the vector product
- then we take its magnitude and multiply by the proton charge

---

## Final Answer

The magnitude of the magnetic force is:

|F| ≈ 1.40 × 10^(-18) N
