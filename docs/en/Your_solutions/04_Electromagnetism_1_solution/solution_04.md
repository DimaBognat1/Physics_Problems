# 4. Force Comparison

## Given

We compare the electric force and the gravitational force between an electron and a proton in a hydrogen atom.

Average distance:

r = 5.3 × 10^(-11) m

Constants:

k = 9.0 × 10^9 N·m²/C²  
e = 1.6 × 10^(-19) C  
G = 6.67 × 10^(-11) N·m²/kg²  
m_e = 9.11 × 10^(-31) kg  
m_p = 1.67 × 10^(-27) kg

We need to find:

1. the electric force F_e
2. the gravitational force F_g
3. the ratio F_e / F_g

---

## Step 1: Electric force

By Coulomb’s law:

F_e = k e² / r²

First calculate e²:

e² = (1.6 × 10^(-19))² = 2.56 × 10^(-38)

Now calculate r²:

r² = (5.3 × 10^(-11))² = 2.809 × 10^(-21)

Substitute into the formula:

F_e = (9.0 × 10^9)(2.56 × 10^(-38)) / (2.809 × 10^(-21))

Now calculate the numerator:

9.0 × 10^9 × 2.56 × 10^(-38) = 2.304 × 10^(-28)

So:

F_e = 2.304 × 10^(-28) / 2.809 × 10^(-21)

F_e ≈ 8.20 × 10^(-8) N

---

## Step 2: Gravitational force

By Newton’s law of gravitation:

F_g = G m_e m_p / r²

First calculate the product of masses:

m_e m_p = (9.11 × 10^(-31))(1.67 × 10^(-27))

m_e m_p ≈ 1.521 × 10^(-57)

Now multiply by G:

G m_e m_p = (6.67 × 10^(-11))(1.521 × 10^(-57))

≈ 1.014 × 10^(-67)

Now divide by r²:

F_g = 1.014 × 10^(-67) / 2.809 × 10^(-21)

F_g ≈ 3.61 × 10^(-47) N

---

## Step 3: Ratio of forces

Now compute:

F_e / F_g = (8.20 × 10^(-8)) / (3.61 × 10^(-47))

F_e / F_g ≈ 2.27 × 10^(39)

---

## Explanation

The electric force and the gravitational force both depend on 1/r², so at the same distance their ratio is determined only by the constants and the particle properties.

The result shows that the electric attraction between the electron and proton is enormously stronger than the gravitational attraction.

This is why gravity is completely negligible at the atomic scale.

---

## Final Answer

Electric force:

F_e ≈ 8.20 × 10^(-8) N

Gravitational force:

F_g ≈ 3.61 × 10^(-47) N

Ratio:

F_e / F_g ≈ 2.27 × 10^(39)
