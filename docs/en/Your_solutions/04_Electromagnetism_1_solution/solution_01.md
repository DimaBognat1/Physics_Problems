# 1. Coulomb's Law

## Given

Four point charges of:

q = +1.0 C

are placed at the corners of a square of side:

a = 1.0 m

A charge of:

Q = −2.0 C

is placed at the center of the square.

We need to calculate:

- the magnitude of the electric force on the central charge
- the direction of the force

---

## Step 1: Distance from the center to each corner

The diagonal of a square is:

d = a√2

So the distance from the center to one corner is half of the diagonal:

r = d / 2 = a√2 / 2

Since a = 1.0 m:

r = √2 / 2 m

---

## Step 2: Force from one corner charge

Using Coulomb's law:

F = k |qQ| / r²

where:

- k = 9 × 10^9 N·m²/C²
- q = 1.0 C
- Q = 2.0 C
- r² = (√2 / 2)² = 1/2

Substitute the values:

F₁ = (9 × 10^9)(1)(2) / (1/2)

F₁ = (18 × 10^9) × 2

F₁ = 36 × 10^9 N

So each corner charge exerts a force of:

F₁ = 3.6 × 10^10 N

on the central charge.

---

## Step 3: Direction of each force

The central charge is negative and all four corner charges are positive.

Therefore, each force is attractive.

So the charge at the center is pulled toward each corner.

---

## Step 4: Use symmetry

The four forces have the same magnitude.

They are directed symmetrically toward the four corners of the square.

For every force in one direction, there is an equal force in the opposite direction.

So the horizontal components cancel out, and the vertical components also cancel out.

That means the vector sum of all four forces is zero.

---

## Step 5: Resultant force

Because of symmetry:

F_net = 0

---

## Explanation

Even though each individual charge exerts a very large attractive force on the central charge, the arrangement is perfectly symmetric.

As a result, all forces cancel each other, so the net force is zero.

---

## Final Answer

Magnitude of the total electric force:

F_net = 0 N

Direction:

There is no direction, because the net force is zero.
