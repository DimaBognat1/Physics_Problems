# 5. Field Levitation

## Given

We need to find the electric field strength required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity.

Mass of proton:

m_p = 1.67 × 10^(-27) kg

Charge of proton:

e = 1.6 × 10^(-19) C

Gravitational acceleration:

g = 9.8 m/s²

---

## Step 1: Condition for levitation

For the proton to levitate, the upward electric force must balance the downward gravitational force.

So:

F_e = F_g

The electric force is:

F_e = qE

The gravitational force is:

F_g = mg

Therefore:

qE = mg

Since the proton charge is e:

eE = mg

---

## Step 2: Solve for electric field

Rearrange the equation:

E = mg / e

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

\[
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
\]

First calculate the numerator:

\[
1.67 \times 10^{-27} \times 9.8 = 1.6366 \times 10^{-26}
\]

Now divide by the charge:

\[
E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
\]

\[
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}
\]

---

## Step 4: Direction of the field

A proton has positive charge, so the electric force acts in the same direction as the electric field.

Since gravity acts downward and we want the electric force upward, the electric field must also point upward.

---

## Explanation

The proton levitates when the upward electric force exactly cancels the downward weight.

Because the proton has a very small mass, the required electric field is also very small.

---

## Final Answer

The required electric field strength is:

\[
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}
\]

Direction of the field:

**upward**
