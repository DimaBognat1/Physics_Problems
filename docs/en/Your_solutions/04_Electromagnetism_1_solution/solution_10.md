# 10. Lorentz Force acting on Wire

## Given

A straight wire has:

Length:

L = 2.0 m

Current:

I = 10 A

Magnetic field:

B = 0.5 T

We need to calculate the magnetic force on the wire for three different angles between the wire and the magnetic field:

a) θ = 90°  
b) θ = 45°  
c) θ = 0°

---

## Step 1: Formula for magnetic force on a current-carrying wire

The magnitude of the magnetic force is:

F = BIL sinθ

where:

- B is the magnetic field
- I is the current
- L is the length of the wire
- θ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field

---

## Step 2: Calculate the common factor

First compute:

BIL = 0.5 × 10 × 2.0 = 10

So the force becomes:

F = 10 sinθ

Now we evaluate this for each angle.

---

## Step 3: Case (a), θ = 90°

We know:

sin90° = 1

So:

F = 10 × 1 = 10 N

---

## Step 4: Case (b), θ = 45°

We know:

sin45° = √2 / 2 ≈ 0.707

So:

F = 10 × 0.707 ≈ 7.07 N

---

## Step 5: Case (c), θ = 0°

We know:

sin0° = 0

So:

F = 10 × 0 = 0 N

---

## Explanation

The magnetic force depends on the angle between the wire and the magnetic field.

- It is maximum when the wire is perpendicular to the field.
- It is zero when the wire is parallel to the field.

That is why the force is:

- largest at 90°
- smaller at 45°
- zero at 0°

---

## Final Answer

a) For θ = 90°:

F = 10 N

b) For θ = 45°:

F ≈ 7.07 N

c) For θ = 0°:

F = 0 N
