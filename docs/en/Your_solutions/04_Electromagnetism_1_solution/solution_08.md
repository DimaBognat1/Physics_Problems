# 8. Lorentz Force

## Given

A charged particle has:

q = 2 × 10^(-19) C  
m = 4 × 10^(-27) kg  

It enters a magnetic field:

B = 0.5 T

with speed:

v = 10^6 m/s

The velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field.

We need to find the magnitude of the Lorentz force.

---

## Step 1: Formula for magnetic Lorentz force

The magnitude of the magnetic part of the Lorentz force is:

F = qvB sinθ

where:
- q is the charge
- v is the speed
- B is the magnetic field
- θ is the angle between velocity and magnetic field

---

## Step 2: Use the fact that the motion is perpendicular

Since the particle enters the field perpendicularly, the angle is:

θ = 90°

and:

sin90° = 1

So the formula becomes:

F = qvB

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

F = (2 × 10^(-19)) × (10^6) × (0.5)

First multiply:

10^6 × 0.5 = 5 × 10^5

Now:

F = (2 × 10^(-19)) × (5 × 10^5)

Multiply the numbers:

2 × 5 = 10

So:

F = 10 × 10^(-14)

F = 1 × 10^(-13) N

---

## Explanation

Because the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, the magnetic force is maximum.

The magnetic field does not change the speed of the particle, only the direction of motion.

That is why the Lorentz force is responsible for bending the trajectory.

---

## Final Answer

The magnitude of the Lorentz force is:

F = 1.0 × 10^(-13) N
