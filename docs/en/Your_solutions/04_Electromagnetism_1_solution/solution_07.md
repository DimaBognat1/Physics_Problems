# 7. Cyclotron Motion

## Given

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference:

V = 5000 V

Then it enters a uniform magnetic field:

B = 0.1 T

The magnetic field is perpendicular to the velocity.

We need to find the radius of the circular path.

Constants:

e = 1.6 × 10^(-19) C  
m_e = 9.11 × 10^(-31) kg

---

## Step 1: Find the speed of the electron after acceleration

The electron starts from rest, so the electric potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.

The gained kinetic energy is:

eV = (1/2)mv²

Solve for v:

v = √(2eV / m)

Substitute the values:

v = √[2 × (1.6 × 10^(-19)) × 5000 / (9.11 × 10^(-31))]

First calculate the numerator:

2 × 1.6 × 10^(-19) × 5000 = 1.6 × 10^(-15)

Now divide by the electron mass:

1.6 × 10^(-15) / 9.11 × 10^(-31) ≈ 1.756 × 10^15

Take the square root:

v ≈ 4.19 × 10^7 m/s

---

## Step 2: Use the formula for circular motion in a magnetic field

When a charged particle enters a magnetic field perpendicularly, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force.

So:

qvB = mv² / r

Solve for r:

r = mv / qB

For an electron:

r = m_e v / eB

Substitute the values:

r = (9.11 × 10^(-31)) × (4.19 × 10^7) / [(1.6 × 10^(-19)) × 0.1]

First calculate the numerator:

9.11 × 10^(-31) × 4.19 × 10^7 ≈ 3.82 × 10^(-23)

Now the denominator:

1.6 × 10^(-19) × 0.1 = 1.6 × 10^(-20)

Now divide:

r ≈ 3.82 × 10^(-23) / 1.6 × 10^(-20)

r ≈ 2.39 × 10^(-3) m

---

## Step 3: Convert to more convenient units

r ≈ 0.00239 m

This is:

r ≈ 2.39 mm

---

## Explanation

The electron first gains speed because it is accelerated by the electric potential difference.

Then, in the magnetic field, the magnetic force is always perpendicular to the velocity, so it does not change the speed, only the direction.

That is why the electron moves in a circular path.

---

## Final Answer

The radius of the circular path is:

r ≈ 2.39 × 10^(-3) m

or

r ≈ 2.39 mm
