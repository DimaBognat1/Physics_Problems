# 10. Average Current

## Given

A lightning bolt transfers charge:

Q = 30 C

The time interval is:

t = 2 ms

We need to find the average current.

---

## Step 1: Formula for average current

Average current is charge divided by time:

I_avg = Q / t

where:

- I_avg is the average current
- Q is the charge
- t is the time interval

---

## Step 2: Convert milliseconds to seconds

The time is given in milliseconds.

1 ms = 10^(-3) s

Therefore:

2 ms = 2 × 10^(-3) s

t = 0.002 s

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

I_avg = Q / t

I_avg = 30 / 0.002

---

## Step 4: Calculate

I_avg = 15000 A

or:

I_avg = 1.5 × 10^4 A

---

## Explanation

A lightning bolt transfers a large amount of charge in a very short time.

Since current is charge per unit time, a small time interval gives a very large current.

---

## Final Answer

The average current of the lightning bolt is:

I_avg = 15000 A

or:

I_avg = 1.5 × 10^4 A
