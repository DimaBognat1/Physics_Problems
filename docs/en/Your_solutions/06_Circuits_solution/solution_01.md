# 1. Series and Parallel Circuit

## Given

We have three resistors:

R₁ = 15 Ω

R₂ = 30 Ω

R₃ = 50 Ω

The battery voltage is:

V = 12 V

We need to calculate:

1. the total equivalent resistance when the resistors are connected in series
2. the current from the battery in the series case
3. the total equivalent resistance when the resistors are connected in parallel
4. the current from the battery in the parallel case

---

## Part 1: Resistors connected in series

## Step 1: Formula for series connection

For resistors connected in series, the equivalent resistance is the sum of all resistances:

R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃

---

## Step 2: Substitute the values

R_total = 15 + 30 + 50

R_total = 95 Ω

So, for the series connection:

R_series = 95 Ω

---

## Step 3: Find the current in the series circuit

Using Ohm's law:

I = V / R

Substitute the values:

I = 12 / 95

I ≈ 0.126 A

So the current from the battery in the series case is:

I_series ≈ 0.126 A

---

## Part 2: Resistors connected in parallel

## Step 4: Formula for parallel connection

For resistors connected in parallel:

1 / R_total = 1 / R₁ + 1 / R₂ + 1 / R₃

Substitute the values:

1 / R_total = 1/15 + 1/30 + 1/50

---

## Step 5: Calculate the equivalent resistance

Use a common denominator 150:

1/15 = 10/150

1/30 = 5/150

1/50 = 3/150

So:

1 / R_total = 10/150 + 5/150 + 3/150

1 / R_total = 18/150

1 / R_total = 0.12

Therefore:

R_total = 1 / 0.12

R_total ≈ 8.33 Ω

So, for the parallel connection:

R_parallel ≈ 8.33 Ω

---

## Step 6: Find the current in the parallel circuit

Again, using Ohm's law:

I = V / R

Substitute the values:

I = 12 / 8.33

I ≈ 1.44 A

So the current from the battery in the parallel case is:

I_parallel ≈ 1.44 A

---

## Explanation

In a series circuit, resistances add directly, so the total resistance becomes larger.

In a parallel circuit, the equivalent resistance becomes smaller than the smallest individual resistor because the current has several paths to flow through.

That is why the current in the parallel circuit is much larger than the current in the series circuit.

---

## Final Answer

For the series connection:

R_series = 95 Ω

I_series ≈ 0.126 A

For the parallel connection:

R_parallel ≈ 8.33 Ω

I_parallel ≈ 1.44 A
