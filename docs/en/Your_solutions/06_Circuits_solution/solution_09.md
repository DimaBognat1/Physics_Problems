# 9. Current

## Given

The charge flowing through the wire is given by:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

We need to find the current at:

t = 3 s

---

## Step 1: Formula for current

Electric current is the rate of change of charge with respect to time:

I(t) = dQ/dt

This means that to find current, we need to take the derivative of Q(t).

---

## Step 2: Differentiate Q(t)

Given:

Q(t) = 5t² + 5

Differentiate:

I(t) = d/dt(5t² + 5)

I(t) = 10t

The constant 5 disappears because the derivative of a constant is zero.

---

## Step 3: Substitute t = 3 s

I(3) = 10 · 3

I(3) = 30 A

---

## Explanation

Charge Q(t) tells us how much charge has passed through the wire by time t.

Current tells us how fast this charge is changing.

At t = 3 s, the charge is increasing at a rate of 30 coulombs per second, so the current is 30 amperes.

---

## Final Answer

The current at t = 3 s is:

I = 30 A
