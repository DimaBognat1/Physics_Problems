# 1. Wave Properties

## Given

Frequency:

f = 440 Hz

Speed of sound in air:

v_air = 343 m/s

Speed of sound in water:

v_water = 1482 m/s

---

## Formula

The wavelength is given by:

\[
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
\]

where:
- \(\lambda\) is the wavelength,
- \(v\) is the wave speed,
- \(f\) is the frequency.

---

## Step 1: Wavelength in air

Substitute the values:

\[
\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}
\]

\[
\lambda_{air} \approx 0.78 \text{ m}
\]

---

## Step 2: Wavelength in water

Substitute the values:

\[
\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}
\]

\[
\lambda_{water} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
\]

---

## Explanation

The frequency stays the same when the wave enters another medium, but the speed changes.  
Since the speed of sound is much greater in water than in air, the wavelength in water is also greater.

---

## Final Answer

Wavelength in air:

\[
\lambda_{air} \approx 0.78 \text{ m}
\]

Wavelength in water:

\[
\lambda_{water} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
\]
