# 6. Wave Equation

## Given

The wave is described by:

y(x,t) = 0.05 sin(2πx − 50πt)

We need to determine:

a) amplitude A  
b) wavelength λ  
c) frequency f  
d) wave speed v  

---

## Step 1: Compare with the standard form

The standard form of a traveling wave is:

y(x,t) = A sin(kx − ωt)

where:

- A is the amplitude
- k is the wave number
- ω is the angular frequency

Now compare:

y(x,t) = 0.05 sin(2πx − 50πt)

So:

A = 0.05  
k = 2π  
ω = 50π  

---

## Step 2: Find the amplitude

The amplitude is the coefficient in front of the sine function.

So:

A = 0.05 m

---

## Step 3: Find the wavelength

We use the relation:

k = 2π / λ

So:

λ = 2π / k

Substitute k = 2π:

λ = 2π / 2π = 1 m

---

## Step 4: Find the frequency

We use the relation:

ω = 2πf

So:

f = ω / 2π

Substitute ω = 50π:

f = 50π / 2π = 25 Hz

---

## Step 5: Find the wave speed

Wave speed is:

v = fλ

Substitute the values:

v = 25 × 1 = 25 m/s

We can also check using:

v = ω / k = 50π / 2π = 25 m/s

---

## Explanation

From the wave equation we directly read the amplitude, wave number, and angular frequency.

Then:

- wavelength comes from k = 2π / λ
- frequency comes from ω = 2πf
- speed comes from v = fλ

---

## Final Answer

Amplitude:

A = 0.05 m

Wavelength:

λ = 1 m

Frequency:

f = 25 Hz

Wave speed:

v = 25 m/s
