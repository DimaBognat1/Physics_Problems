# 1. Gauss's Law

## Given

A point charge is located at the origin:

q = +2 C

A spherical surface has radius:

r = 1 m

The sphere is centered at the origin.

We need to calculate the electric flux through the spherical surface.

---

## Step 1: Use Gauss's Law

Gauss's Law states that the total electric flux through a closed surface is equal to the enclosed charge divided by the vacuum permittivity:

Φ_E = q_enclosed / ε₀

where:

- Φ_E is the electric flux
- q_enclosed is the charge inside the closed surface
- ε₀ is the vacuum permittivity

The value of vacuum permittivity is:

ε₀ = 8.85 × 10^(-12) C²/(N·m²)

---

## Step 2: Identify the enclosed charge

The charge is placed at the center of the spherical surface.

Therefore, the whole charge is inside the sphere:

q_enclosed = +2 C

The radius of the sphere does not change the total flux, because Gauss's Law depends only on the enclosed charge.

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

Φ_E = q_enclosed / ε₀

Φ_E = 2 / (8.85 × 10^(-12))

Now calculate:

Φ_E ≈ 2.26 × 10^11 N·m²/C

---

## Explanation

The electric field strength depends on the distance from the charge.

However, the total electric flux through a closed surface depends only on the charge enclosed by that surface.

Since the charge is positive, the electric flux is also positive. This means the electric field lines leave the spherical surface outward.

---

## Final Answer

The electric flux through the spherical surface is:

Φ_E ≈ 2.26 × 10^11 N·m²/C

The flux is positive because the enclosed charge is positive.
