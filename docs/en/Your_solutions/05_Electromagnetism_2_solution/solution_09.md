# 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Given

A light ray travels from air into glass.

The refractive index of air is:

n₁ = 1.00

The refractive index of glass is:

n₂ = 1.50

The angle of incidence is:

θ₁ = 30°

We need to find the angle of refraction θ₂.

---

## Step 1: Use Snell's Law

Snell's Law is:

n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂

where:

- n₁ is the refractive index of the first medium
- n₂ is the refractive index of the second medium
- θ₁ is the angle of incidence
- θ₂ is the angle of refraction

---

## Step 2: Solve for sin θ₂

Rearrange the formula:

sin θ₂ = n₁ sin θ₁ / n₂

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values

sin θ₂ = (1.00 × sin 30°) / 1.50

Since:

sin 30° = 0.5

we get:

sin θ₂ = (1.00 × 0.5) / 1.50

sin θ₂ = 0.5 / 1.5

sin θ₂ = 0.333

---

## Step 4: Find θ₂

Now take the inverse sine:

θ₂ = sin⁻¹(0.333)

θ₂ ≈ 19.5°

---

## Explanation

The light ray goes from air into glass.

Glass has a larger refractive index than air, so the light slows down and bends toward the normal.

That is why the angle of refraction is smaller than the angle of incidence.

---

## Final Answer

The angle of refraction is:

θ₂ ≈ 19.5°
