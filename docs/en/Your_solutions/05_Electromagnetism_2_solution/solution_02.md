# 2. Ampere's Law

## Given

Two long parallel wires are separated by a distance:

d = 10 cm = 0.10 m

Each wire carries current:

I = 5 A

The currents flow in opposite directions.

We need to find the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at the point midway between the wires.

---

## Step 1: Distance from each wire to the midpoint

The point is exactly halfway between the wires.

Therefore, the distance from each wire to the point is:

r = d / 2

r = 0.10 / 2 = 0.05 m

---

## Step 2: Magnetic field from one long straight wire

The magnetic field produced by a long straight wire is:

B = μ₀I / (2πr)

where:

μ₀ = 4π × 10^(-7) T·m/A

---

## Step 3: Substitute the values for one wire

B₁ = (4π × 10^(-7) × 5) / (2π × 0.05)

Simplify:

B₁ = (20π × 10^(-7)) / (0.1π)

B₁ = 2.0 × 10^(-5) T

So the magnetic field from one wire at the midpoint is:

B₁ = 2.0 × 10^(-5) T

---

## Step 4: Add the magnetic fields

Because the currents flow in opposite directions, the magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the same direction.

Therefore, the total field is:

B_total = B₁ + B₂

Since both wires carry the same current and the midpoint is the same distance from both wires:

B₂ = B₁

So:

B_total = 2B₁

B_total = 2 × 2.0 × 10^(-5)

B_total = 4.0 × 10^(-5) T

---

## Step 5: Direction of the magnetic field

The direction is found using the right-hand rule.

Since the currents are in opposite directions, the magnetic fields between the wires reinforce each other.

So the magnetic field at the midpoint is perpendicular to the plane containing the two wires.

The exact direction, into or out of the page, depends on which wire has current upward and which has current downward.

---

## Explanation

For one straight wire, the magnetic field decreases with distance as:

B = μ₀I / (2πr)

At the midpoint, the point is equally distant from both wires.

Because the currents are opposite, the magnetic field contributions between the wires have the same direction, so they add.

---

## Final Answer

The magnitude of the magnetic field at the midpoint is:

B_total = 4.0 × 10^(-5) T

The direction is perpendicular to the plane of the wires, determined by the right-hand rule.
