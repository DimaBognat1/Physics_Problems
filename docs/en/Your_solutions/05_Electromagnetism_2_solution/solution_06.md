# 6. EM Wave Analysis

## Given

An electromagnetic wave has its electric field component:

E_y(x,t) = 100 sin(10^7 x − ωt) V/m

We need to find:

1. the direction of propagation
2. the wavelength λ
3. the angular frequency ω
4. the magnetic field component

---

## Step 1: Compare with the standard wave equation

A traveling wave moving in the positive x-direction has the form:

E(x,t) = E_0 sin(kx − ωt)

Our wave is:

E_y(x,t) = 100 sin(10^7 x − ωt)

So we can identify:

E_0 = 100 V/m

k = 10^7 rad/m

Because the argument is kx − ωt, the wave propagates in the positive x-direction.

---

## Step 2: Find the wavelength

The wave number k is related to wavelength by:

k = 2π / λ

So:

λ = 2π / k

Substitute the value:

λ = 2π / 10^7

λ ≈ 6.28 × 10^(-7) m

So:

λ ≈ 628 nm

---

## Step 3: Find the angular frequency

For an electromagnetic wave in vacuum or air:

ω = ck

where:

c = 3.0 × 10^8 m/s

Substitute the values:

ω = (3.0 × 10^8)(10^7)

ω = 3.0 × 10^15 rad/s

---

## Step 4: Find the magnetic field amplitude

For an electromagnetic wave:

E_0 = cB_0

So:

B_0 = E_0 / c

Substitute the values:

B_0 = 100 / (3.0 × 10^8)

B_0 ≈ 3.33 × 10^(-7) T

---

## Step 5: Determine the direction of the magnetic field

The electric field is in the y-direction.

The wave propagates in the positive x-direction.

For an electromagnetic wave, the directions of E, B, and propagation are perpendicular to each other.

We need:

E × B = direction of propagation

Since:

y × z = x

the magnetic field must be in the z-direction.

---

## Final Answer

The wave propagates in the positive x-direction.

The wavelength is:

λ ≈ 6.28 × 10^(-7) m = 628 nm

The angular frequency is:

ω = 3.0 × 10^15 rad/s

The magnetic field component is:

B_z(x,t) = 3.33 × 10^(-7) sin(10^7 x − 3.0 × 10^15 t) T
