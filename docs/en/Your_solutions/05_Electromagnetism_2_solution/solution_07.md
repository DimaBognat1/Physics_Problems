# 7. Wavelength and Frequency

## Given

The human eye is most sensitive to light with wavelength:

λ = 550 nm

We need to determine:

1. the color of this light
2. the frequency of this light

The speed of light is:

c = 3.0 × 10^8 m/s

---

## Step 1: Identify the color

A wavelength of about 550 nm belongs to the visible spectrum.

This wavelength corresponds approximately to green light.

So the color is:

green

---

## Step 2: Convert wavelength to meters

The wavelength is given in nanometers.

1 nm = 10^(-9) m

Therefore:

λ = 550 nm = 550 × 10^(-9) m

λ = 5.5 × 10^(-7) m

---

## Step 3: Use the wave formula

The relationship between wave speed, wavelength, and frequency is:

c = λf

Solve for frequency:

f = c / λ

---

## Step 4: Substitute the values

f = (3.0 × 10^8) / (5.5 × 10^(-7))

Now calculate:

f ≈ 5.45 × 10^14 Hz

---

## Explanation

Visible light with wavelength around 550 nm is perceived as green by the human eye.

The frequency is very high because visible light has a very small wavelength.

---

## Final Answer

The wavelength 550 nm corresponds approximately to:

green light

The frequency is:

f ≈ 5.45 × 10^14 Hz
