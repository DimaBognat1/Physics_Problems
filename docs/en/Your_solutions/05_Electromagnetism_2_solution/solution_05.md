# 5. Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Given

We have a parallel-plate capacitor with the following parameters:

S = 0.02 m²

d = 5 mm = 0.005 m

U = 500 V

We need to calculate:

1. the capacitance C of the capacitor
2. the energy stored in the capacitor
3. the electric field intensity E between the plates
4. the force of attraction F between the plates

The vacuum permittivity is:

ε₀ = 8.85 × 10^(-12) F/m

---

## Step 1: Capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor

The formula for capacitance is:

C = ε₀S / d

Substitute the values:

C = (8.85 × 10^(-12) × 0.02) / 0.005

First calculate:

0.02 / 0.005 = 4

So:

C = 8.85 × 10^(-12) × 4

C = 3.54 × 10^(-11) F

---

## Step 2: Energy stored in the capacitor

The energy stored in a capacitor is:

W = 1/2 CU²

Substitute the values:

W = 1/2 × 3.54 × 10^(-11) × 500²

Now:

500² = 250000

So:

W = 0.5 × 3.54 × 10^(-11) × 250000

W = 4.425 × 10^(-6) J

So approximately:

W ≈ 4.43 × 10^(-6) J

---

## Step 3: Electric field intensity between the plates

For a parallel-plate capacitor:

E = U / d

Substitute the values:

E = 500 / 0.005

E = 100000 V/m

So:

E = 1.0 × 10^5 V/m

---

## Step 4: Force of attraction between the plates

The force between capacitor plates can be calculated using:

F = 1/2 ε₀ E² S

Substitute the values:

F = 1/2 × 8.85 × 10^(-12) × (1.0 × 10^5)² × 0.02

First:

(1.0 × 10^5)² = 1.0 × 10^10

So:

F = 1/2 × 8.85 × 10^(-12) × 1.0 × 10^10 × 0.02

F = 8.85 × 10^(-4) N

---

## Explanation

The capacitance depends on the plate area and the distance between the plates.

A larger area gives larger capacitance, while a larger distance gives smaller capacitance.

The electric field is the voltage divided by the plate separation.

The plates attract each other because opposite charges are stored on them.

---

## Final Answer

Capacitance:

C = 3.54 × 10^(-11) F

Energy stored:

W ≈ 4.43 × 10^(-6) J

Electric field intensity:

E = 1.0 × 10^5 V/m

Force of attraction:

F = 8.85 × 10^(-4) N
