# Section 1 — Elimination of Time

We are given:

x(t) = 2t²  
y(t) = 3t³  

---

## Solution

### Step 1: Eliminate parameter

t² = x / 2  

t = √(x/2)  

Substitute into y:

y = 3t³ = 3(t²·t)  

y = 3(x/2)√(x/2)  

---

### Step 2: Velocity

v(t) = (dx/dt, dy/dt)

dx/dt = 4t  
dy/dt = 9t²  

v(t) = (4t, 9t²)

---

### Step 3: Acceleration

a(t) = (4, 18t)

---

### Step 4: Is acceleration constant?

No, because it depends on t.

---

## Final Answer

Trajectory: y = 3(x/2)√(x/2)  

v(t) = (4t, 9t²)  
a(t) = (4, 18t)  

Acceleration is not constant.
