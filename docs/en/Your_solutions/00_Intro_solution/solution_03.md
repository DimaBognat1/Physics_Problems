# Section 0 — Proportionality

We are given the formula of gravitational force:

F = G * (m₁m₂) / r²  

We need to determine how the force changes if:
- distance r is doubled  
- masses m₁ and m₂ are halved  

---

## Solution

### Step 1: Effect of doubling distance

r → 2r  

F ∝ 1 / r²  

So:

F → 1 / (2r)² = 1 / (4r²)

Result: force becomes 1/4 of the original

---

### Step 2: Effect of halving masses

m₁ → m₁/2  
m₂ → m₂/2  

m₁m₂ → (m₁m₂) / 4  

Result: force becomes 1/4 of the original

---

### Step 3: Combine both effects

Total change:

F → (1/4) * (1/4) = 1/16  

---

## Final Answer

The force decreases by a factor of 16.
